Bhalaydhunga Roapway

Bhaley Dhunga, also known as Bhalay Dhunga, is a prominent cliff located near Yangang in South Sikkim, India. Perched at an elevation of approximately 3,300 meters, it offers a panoramic 360-degree view of the Himalayan range and the surrounding valleys. The name "Bhaley Dhunga" translates to "Bhaley's Rock" in the local language. Traditionally, reaching the summit of Bhaley Dhunga required a challenging trek of about six hours through alpine forests rich in biodiversity. Trekkers would traverse snow-covered trails, especially during certain seasons, making the journey both demanding and rewarding. The trek provides an opportunity to disconnect from daily life and immerse oneself in nature's tranquility. To enhance accessibility and boost tourism, a passenger ropeway connecting Dhapper to Bhaley Dhunga peak was inaugurated on September 16, 2024. This 3.5 km long ropeway, considered one of the top five toughest in the world due to its steep 60-degree slope, reduces the travel time to the peak to approximately 13 minutes. The ropeway features 18 gondolas, each with a capacity of eight passengers, and offers a thrilling ascent with breathtaking views. The introduction of the ropeway has made Bhaley Dhunga more accessible to tourists, allowing them to experience the majestic views without the strenuous trek. The ticket price for the ropeway is set at 950 rupees. Yangang, the town near Bhaley Dhunga, is also home to other natural attractions such as the serene Tig-Day Cho Lake and the glittering Neya Khola Falls. These sites, combined with the new ropeway, position Yangang as a burgeoning destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers in Sikkim. For those interested in experiencing the ropeway, it starts from Dhapper, approximately 5 km above Yangang Bazaar, making it a convenient addition to the region's tourism infrastructure. Whether you choose to trek the traditional route or opt for the ropeway, Bhaley Dhunga offers a unique blend of adventure and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination in Sikkim.

Yangang Helipad

The Yangang Helipad, located in Yangang, South Sikkim, serves as a significant landmark in the region. Beyond its primary function as a landing site for helicopters, it has become a popular spot for locals and visitors alike, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes. Over time, the helipad has evolved into a community hub, hosting various events and activities. For instance, a five-day Aamsha healing camp was organized at the helipad, providing participants with wellness sessions in a serene environment. Additionally, the expansive open space of the helipad makes it an ideal location for recreational activities, such as setting up tents for camping. However, recent reports have highlighted concerns regarding the helipad's maintenance. A video shared on social media showcased its deteriorating condition, emphasizing the need for timely upkeep to preserve its utility and appeal. Despite these challenges, the Yangang Helipad remains a cherished spot, especially during sunrise, offering breathtaking views that captivate photographers and nature enthusiasts. In summary, the Yangang Helipad stands as more than just an aviation facility; it is a community space that offers recreational opportunities and scenic vistas, contributing to the charm of South Sikkim.

Tig-Day-Cho (Lake)

Near the Yangang Helipad in South Sikkim lies the serene Tig-Day Cho Lake, also referred to as Tik-dey-cho. This sacred lake is encircled by dense forests, offering visitors a tranquil environment to relax and connect with nature. The area is a haven for birdwatchers, with the surrounding forests home to a variety of Himalayan bird species. However, it's important to note that there have been concerns about litter and trash around the lake. Visitors are encouraged to maintain the sanctity of this site by ensuring they do not contribute to the pollution and by participating in clean-up efforts if possible.

Nehya Falls

Neya Falls, located approximately 5 kilometers from Yangang in South Sikkim, is a picturesque waterfall renowned for its serene ambiance and natural beauty. The cascading waters create a soothing atmosphere, making it a favored spot for both locals and tourists seeking tranquility amidst nature. The area surrounding Neya Falls is characterized by lush greenery, offering visitors a refreshing escape from urban life. The site is accessible via a short drive from Yangang town, followed by a brief walk through verdant trails leading to the waterfall. Neya Falls is an ideal destination for nature enthusiasts, photographers, and those looking to experience the serene beauty of South Sikkim. The combination of the waterfall's cascading waters and the surrounding natural environment provides a peaceful retreat for visitors.

Sog Yungdrung Ling Bon Monastery

Sog Yungdrung Ling Bon Monastery, established in 2013 by Khenpo Yonten Gyatso Rinpoche, is situated near Yangang in South Sikkim, India. The monastery serves as a sanctuary for over 80-90 children from underprivileged backgrounds, offering them education, shelter, and support. Its mission encompasses not only the preservation of the Bon traditionthe indigenous religion of Tibetbut also the holistic development of its residents, aiming to instill ethical values and provide life skills alongside traditional education. The monastery complex is nestled in a remote, mountainous region, providing a serene environment conducive to learning and spiritual growth. The children, many of whom are orphans or come from families unable to support them, receive education in various subjects, including language, mathematics, and science, as well as life skills such as hygiene and nutrition. Spiritual education in the Bon tradition is also a significant aspect of their curriculum. Sog Yungdrung Ling Bon Monastery relies on donations from individuals and organizations worldwide to continue its operations and support its residents. The monastery also welcomes volunteers who wish to contribute their time and skills to assist the children. In addition to its educational endeavors, the monastery actively participates in cultural and religious events. For instance, the students gather to celebrate the birthday of His Holiness the Dalai Lama, reflecting their devotion and the monastery's role in preserving Tibetan cultural practices. Pathing Landslide

Pathing, a village located between Yangang and Ravangla in South Sikkim, has been experiencing significant landslide activity for over three decades. The area is predominantly inhabited by Gurung agriculturists who have faced ongoing challenges due to the instability of the terrain. In November 2022, the situation escalated when a massive landslide struck the village, leading to the evacuation of at least 60 families. The landslide caused continuous rockfalls, even after the monsoon season had ended, indicating heightened instability. This event severely impacted road connectivity between Ravangla and Yangang, disrupted local agriculture, and adversely affected the education of children in the area. Despite the longstanding nature of the landslide, the exact cause remains unclear. Recent observations suggest that the activity has intensified, with increased rockfalls occurring post-monsoon. The community has suffered significant losses, including damage to farmland and setbacks to the local tourism industry. Efforts have been made to document and raise awareness about the situation in Pathing. Organizations like SaveTheHills, in collaboration with Junkeri Studios, have produced documentaries and photo essays to highlight the impact of the landslide and garner support for the affected communities.
